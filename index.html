The Critical Safety Challenge

Understanding the scope and impact of weather-related motorway incidents

Queensland's motorway network faces significant safety challenges during adverse weather conditions. Our comprehensive analysis reveals the urgent need for weather-responsive traffic management solutions.

The Numbers Tell the Story

  • 204 casualty crashes occurred under wet or rainy conditions
  • 83 Fatal and Serious Injury (FSI) crashes during the same period
  • $138.96 million in estimated economic costs
  • 5.5-year study period (2018 to June 2023)

Current VSL Systems Fall Short

Existing Variable Speed Limit systems are not optimized for real-time weather responses, leaving a critical gap in motorway safety management during adverse conditions.

The WiSe Solution

Innovative Weather-responsive Variable Speed Limit technology

How Weather-VSL Works

The WiSe project develops an intelligent framework that adjusts speed limits in real-time based on weather conditions, creating a proactive safety system rather than reactive responses.

Expected Benefits

  • Reduced crash rates during adverse weather
  • Improved traffic flow management
  • Enhanced safety for all road users
  • Economic savings from prevented crashes

Research Objectives

A systematic approach to implementing Weather-VSL technology

1

Systematic Review

Perform a systematic review of national and international best practices for Weather-VSL through stakeholder interviews, case studies, and literature.

2

Safety Evaluation

Evaluate motorway safety and operations under adverse weather conditions to ascertain the current operational challenges and opportunities for Weather-VSL implementation.

3

Traffic Modeling

Model traffic flow breakdown probability under adverse weather conditions to understand critical conditions for implementing Weather-VSL.

4

Framework Development

Develop a Weather-VSL framework to adjust speed limits based on real-time weather data.
