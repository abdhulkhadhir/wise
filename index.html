About the Project

Advanced motorway control systems for safer travel in adverse weather conditions

Enhancing Road Safety Through Innovation

The WiSe project represents a collaborative effort between Queensland University of Technology (QUT) and Transport and Main Roads Queensland (TMR) to develop next-generation motorway control systems.

Our research focuses on implementing weather-responsive variable speed limit systems that dynamically adjust speed limits based on real-time weather conditions, significantly improving road safety and traffic flow efficiency.

Research Objectives

Four key focus areas driving our innovative approach

Systematic Review

Perform systematic review of national and international best practices for Weather-VSL through stakeholder interviews, case studies, and literature analysis

Safety Evaluation

Evaluate motorway safety and operations under adverse weather conditions to identify current challenges and implementation opportunities

Traffic Modeling

Model traffic flow breakdown probability under adverse weather conditions to understand critical implementation thresholds

Framework Development

Develop comprehensive Weather-VSL framework for dynamic speed limit adjustment based on real-time weather data
